El Greco

The artist of the Spanish Renaissance

El Greco's history:

  • 1541: Born in the Kingdom of Candia (modern Crete) from a prosperous urban family.
  • 1563: At the age of 22, El Greco was described in a document as a "master" of the guild and presumably operating his own workshop.
  • 1566: Signed his name in Greek as μαΐστρος Μένεγος Θεοτοκόπουλος σγουράφος (maḯstros Ménegos Theotokópoulos sgouráfos; "Master Ménegos Theotokópoulos, painter").
  • 1567: Most scholars agree that El Greco went to Venice.
  • 1570: Moved to Rome and executed a series of works strongly marked by his Venetian apprenticeship.
  • 1572: Opened his own workshop in Rome.
  • 1577: Migrated to Madrid, then to Toledo, where he produced his mature works.
  • 1578: His only son, Jorge Manuel, is born. He also became a painter, assisted his father, and continued to repeat his compositions for many years after he inherited the studio.
  • 1579: Completed nine paintings for Santo Domingo, including The Trinity and The Assumption of the Virgin, establishing the painter's reputation in Toledo.
  • 1597: A period of intense activity for El Greco began, whenhe received several major commissions, and his workshop created pictorial and sculptural ensembles for a variety of religious institutions.
  • 1585: El Greco made Toledo his home, in a complex consisting of three apartments and twenty-four rooms.
  • 1614: Fell seriously ill, and died a month later.
